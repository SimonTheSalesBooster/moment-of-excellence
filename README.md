# Moment of Excellence — The Coaching Move That Changes Everything

**Find the 3 perfect strokes in a 2000m race. Build from those. Not from the other 597.**

This is how elite rowing coaches train world champions. It is also the most underused technique in business coaching, sales debrief, and Monday strategy sessions.

This repo gives you the full technique as a free Claude Code skill — plus the research and psychology behind it.

---

## The Problem With Standard Coaching

Most coaching starts with: "What's the problem this week?"

The problem is familiar. The client has been thinking about it since Tuesday. They arrive in the problem-state — stressed, defensive, focused on what's broken. You set goals from that state.

Then you wonder why follow-through rates are low.

## The Better Opening

Ask this instead:

> "Tell me about one moment in the last seven days where you were genuinely at your best. Even for 90 seconds. What was happening? What were you doing differently?"

Watch what happens to the room.

The physiology shifts. Posture opens. Voice slows. Eyes change. The client re-enters their peak state — not because you praised them, but because you made them locate and re-experience the precise moment where everything worked.

Now you set goals from there.

That is the Moment of Excellence.

---

## Where It Comes From

Three sources built this technique:

**1. Elite sports coaching** — Bernhard Sieber (Austrian Olympic rower, U23 World Champion, Rio 2016) applies this in his Flooozone coaching methodology. Rowing coaches film every race, rewind to the 3 seconds where the boat moved as one, and coach from that moment. Not from the failure. From the 3 perfect strokes.

**2. NLP anchoring** (Bandler & Grinder) — A stimulus linked to a peak physiological state can be retrieved on demand. Every time a client re-enters their Moment of Excellence in full sensory detail, the neural pathway strengthens. Eventually they can access the state before any high-stakes conversation.

**3. Maslow's Peak Experiences (1964)** — Moments of extraordinary performance leave crystalline memory traces. They are data-rich and underexplored. The Peak Interview (Bodynamic) structures how to mine them.

## How to Use It — Four Contexts

**Monday Strategy Sessions (5-8 min at the start)**
Before any agenda item: run Moves 1-5. The week's focus is built from the Moment of Excellence, not from scratch.

**Sales Call Debrief**
Before the score and the fix list: locate the 30-second window where the rep was at their best. Extract the pattern. The rep hears their own excellence before they hear the corrections. The corrections land harder.

**When a Client Is Stuck**
Standard coaching: "Let's problem-solve." Moment of Excellence coaching: "When have you solved something like this before? Take me into that." Shift the state first. Then problem-solve.

**Quarterly Deep-Dive (45 min)**
The full Peak Interview — explore the runway (what was different about that week?), the peak moment, and the extraction (what enabled it, which conditions can you recreate?). Output: three replicable conditions that become the client's pre-performance checklist.

## The 10 Markers of a True Peak Experience

When your client describes an experience with 6+ of these, you've found the real one. Go deep:

1. Rarely shared with others — it carries vulnerability
2. Crystalline recall despite time passing
3. Time felt suspended or expanded
4. Sense of expanded space
5. Heightened sensory awareness
6. Bliss or heart-opening quality
7. Specific physical sensations in the body
8. Deeper conviction or sense of meaning
9. Effortless action — flow
10. Sense of connectedness — to teammates, the work, something larger

## The Psychology (Brief Version)

**State-dependency.** Resources accessed in a peak state are stored with that state. Re-entering the state re-activates the competencies and beliefs — before any advice is given.

**Unconscious competence made conscious.** Peak performers often cannot articulate what they do brilliantly at the moment they're doing it. This technique surfaces tacit knowledge and makes it teachable.

**Neuroplasticity.** Visualizing a peak experience with full sensory detail activates the same neural pathways as the original event. Repeated retrieval strengthens those pathways.

**Flow conditions (Csikszentmihalyi).** The Peak Interview extracts the conditions that triggered flow. Now the client can recreate them deliberately rather than waiting for chance.
