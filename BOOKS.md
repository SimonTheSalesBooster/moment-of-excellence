# Further Reading — The Science Behind the Technique

Ten books that show how peak-state coaching works in sports and business. Ranked by direct relevance to the Moment of Excellence technique: identifying, anchoring, and replicating the conditions of peak performance.

---

## 1. Turtles All the Way Down: Prerequisites to Personal Genius
**John Grinder & Judith DeLozier · 1987 · ISBN: 9780929514017**

The original source. The **Circle of Excellence** — the direct parent of Moment of Excellence — is formally introduced and demonstrated here. This is the transcript and notes from a 5-day seminar Grinder and DeLozier ran in San Francisco in 1986, called "Prerequisites to Personal Genius." The book represents the birth of what they called **New Code NLP**: a significant upgrade from the original NLP that added two things the Classic Code lacked — formal engagement of the unconscious mind, and ecological consideration of consequences before any change is installed.

**The Circle of Excellence — how it works:**
1. Identify an upcoming high-stakes situation where you want a specific resource state
2. Imagine a circle on the floor large enough to step into
3. Recall a memory where you had that state at full intensity — relive it sensorially
4. Step physically into the circle at peak intensity; feel the state surrounding your body
5. Step out; shake out; return to neutral
6. Test: re-enter the circle — does the state return?
7. Stack: repeat with additional resource states to compound the anchor
8. Future pace: imagine the triggering cue of your challenging situation, then step into the circle — the anxiety trigger becomes the state trigger

The mechanism is spatial anchoring: a physical location becomes the stimulus for a resourceful state. Once installed, stepping into any marked or imagined version of the circle fires the state. Grinder's insight was to make the anchor **kinesthetic and spatial rather than purely mental** — the body leads, and the state follows.

**Other key techniques in this book:**
- **Triple Description (Perceptual Positions)**: Routinely occupy three viewpoints — self, other, observer — before decisions. Borrowed from Bateson's systems thinking; trains the mind to perceive consequences at the level of the whole system, not just the individual.
- **Somatic Syntax** (DeLozier's contribution): "Knowledge is only rumor until it is in the muscle." Resource states are accessed and deepened through movement — exploring body posture, rhythm, direction, and quality to find which physical adjustments intensify or diminish the state. The Dancing S.C.O.R.E. technique has practitioners physically walk through Symptoms → Causes → Outcomes → Resources → Effects, discovering what movement pattern belongs at each location.

**Why this book vs. later NLP books:** Every subsequent resource-state technique — including Moment of Excellence — derives the anchoring mechanism from this work. Going here first gives you the operating principle without the interpretive layer added by later writers. Grinder and DeLozier are sparse and precise; they show the technique rather than explain it to death.

**The underlying philosophy in one sentence:** Build the state first, then enter the situation — never let the situation determine your state.

## 2. Flow in Sports: The Keys to Optimal Experiences and Performances
**Susan A. Jackson & Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi · 1999 · ISBN: 9780880118767**

The only book devoted exclusively to flow states in sport. Jackson's qualitative research with elite athletes describes exactly what peak-state feels like, what triggers it, and how coaches recreate those conditions. Csikszentmihalyi provides the theory; Jackson supplies the sports cases. The closest academic parallel to what the Moment of Excellence technique does psychologically.

---

## 3. The Inner Game of Tennis: The Classic Guide to the Mental Side of Peak Performance
**W. Timothy Gallwey · 1974 · ISBN: 9780679778318**

The foundational coaching text on quieting the judging mind to let peak competence perform. Gallwey coaches from excellence — he has players feel what the correct stroke feels like and anchor it, rather than correcting faults. Applied directly to business via *The Inner Game of Work* and used at Apple, AT&T, and Coca-Cola. The most influential predecessor to Moment of Excellence coaching philosophy.

---

## 4. The Art of Learning: An Inner Journey to Optimal Performance
**Josh Waitzkin · 2007 · ISBN: 9780743277464**

Chess prodigy turned world Tai Chi champion. Waitzkin describes in precise detail how he learned to access peak states on demand — through what he calls "Investment in Loss" and building trigger routines that collapse full preparation into a condensed pre-performance cue. His method is a practical implementation of the NLP anchor. Cases span chess, martial arts, and business. The most readable and immediately usable book on this list.

## 5. Coaching with NLP: How to Be a Master Coach
**Joseph O'Connor & Andrea Lages · 2004 · ISBN: 9780007151226**

The primary reference for NLP anchoring applied in coaching sessions. Four-part structure (what coaching is, the coaching process, coaching in practice, resources) built around ten chapters — each ending with action steps for the coachee to implement between sessions. One of the first books to formally combine business coaching methodology with NLP state-work tools including the Circle of Excellence.

**Core NLP techniques with practical mechanics:**

**Resource Anchoring (5 steps):**
1. Identify the state you want to access — confidence, clarity, control
2. Access a memory where you had that state at full intensity; move through it sensorially (VAKOG: what did you see, hear, feel, smell, taste?)
3. Set the anchor *only at peak intensity* — never before, never after the high point
4. The anchor: a distinct gesture, word in a specific tone, or physical sensation — something you can reproduce exactly
5. Break state, then test: fire the anchor; does the state return? If yes, anchored. If partial, stack a second peak memory into the same anchor

**The Outcome Frame (vs. Problem Frame):** Standard coaching asks "what's the problem?" — which puts the client in a problem-state before they've named a goal. O'Connor and Lages instead build a **Well-Formed Outcome** first: what do you want (not what you don't want), stated positively, described in sensory terms, with a specific timeframe and the client as the agent of their own result. This reframe alone changes the coaching state completely — and the coach's job becomes mobilizing resources toward the outcome, not analyzing the history of the problem.

**The 8-stage coaching session structure:**
1. Beginning Frame — clarify purpose and focus
2. State — ensure both coach and client are grounded and resourceful (not stressed, not distracted)
3. Outcome/Direction — define what the client wants from this session
4. Rapport — build psychological safety (rapport is an ongoing calibration, not a one-time act)
5. Current Situation — explore with precision and curiosity (Meta Model questions to surface limiting language patterns)
6. Technique/Task — apply the NLP technique most suited to what emerged
7. Future Pace — mentally rehearse the desired state in the real situation; embed change before the session ends
8. End Frame — confirm learnings, name commitments, close cleanly

**Perceptual Positions — the sales-critical technique:**
- First Position: your own perspective, fully associated
- Second Position: you inhabit the prospect's perspective — see through their eyes, feel their constraints, hear your offer from their position. O'Connor writes that great salespeople and negotiators live in Second Position.
- Third Position: neutral observer — you see both yourself and the other person from outside. Useful when emotionally charged; restores objectivity without losing empathy.

**Sales application of anchoring:** In a discovery call, ask a question that activates a desired buying state ("Tell me about a time you made a change and it worked out better than expected"). Watch for peak signals — eyes lighting up, posture opening, voice slowing. At that peak, fire a subtle anchor: a distinct nod and pause, or a specific phrase in a particular tone. When the time comes to present the offer, re-fire the anchor. The client re-enters the resourceful state, not because you manipulated them, but because you helped them access a state they already have.

**Why this book vs. other NLP coaching books:** Most NLP books (Bandler, Robbins) focus on techniques in isolation. O'Connor and Lages embed those techniques inside a full coaching session structure — so you understand not just *what* to do but *when in the session arc* each technique belongs. For anyone doing weekly coaching sessions with B2B founders, this sequencing intelligence is the gap the other books leave.

## 6. Sporting Excellence: Optimising Sports Performance Using NLP
**Ted Garratt · 1998 · Crown House · ISBN: 9781899836260**

The most direct NLP-in-sport application book. Ted Garratt — founder of Target Resources, an HR and training consultancy, and a certified NLP trainer — takes the full toolkit of Bandler and Grinder and rebuilds it inside a sport-specific self-coaching programme. Every chapter ends with "Activity" boxes: concrete exercises you complete, not just read. Kevin Keegan and Dean Richards contribute real-world input from their coaching careers.

**The book's four-section structure:**

- **Section 1 — Background:** Sport psychology and NLP fundamentals. Garratt opens by distinguishing NLP from mere positive thinking — the argument is that NLP changes the structure of your internal representations (submodalities, anchors, strategies), which positive thinking does not. Establishes the theoretical grounding so the exercises in Section 2 have a mechanism behind them, not just instruction.

- **Section 2 — Practical Application:** The core of the book. Activity boxes introduce established NLP techniques (Circle of Excellence, SWISH) alongside Garratt's sport-adapted versions. Mental warm-up, mental rehearsal, psyching up, recognising signals of success, managing pre-performance anxiety — each covered with step-by-step exercises.

- **Section 3 — Building and Improving:** Chapter 10: Meta Programmes — how different athletes (and different buyers) filter information, make decisions, and get motivated. Chapter 11: Modelling for success — reverse-engineering the internal strategies of elite performers. Chapter 15: Submodalities and attention to effectiveness — adjusting the sensory qualities (brightness, volume, distance) of mental representations to shift emotional response.

- **Section 4 — Self-Coaching:** Brings the full programme into an independent daily practice the athlete runs on themselves without a coach present.

**The two most directly useful techniques for business:**

**SWISH Pattern — replacing automatic negative reactions:**
1. Identify the trigger image that fires the unwanted state (e.g., the prospect's face going flat, the silence after naming the price)
2. Make that image large, bright, and close — feel the reaction it produces
3. Create a replacement image: how you *want* to respond — confident, grounded, in control. Make it small, dim, and distant
4. Simultaneously shrink and push the trigger image away while pulling the replacement image forward — fast, with an audible "swish"
5. Repeat 5–7 times until the trigger no longer fires the unwanted state
6. Test: deliberately bring up the trigger image — notice whether the replacement now fires automatically

*Sport application:* A sprinter who tenses at the starting gun uses the Swish until the gun becomes the cue for a calm, explosive launch. *Business application:* A rep who goes quiet when a prospect challenges the price uses the Swish until price challenge becomes the cue for steady curiosity, not defensiveness.

**Signals of Success — recognition training:**
Most performers can recognise failure states clearly but cannot reliably identify when they're *in flow*. Garratt's signal recognition exercises train athletes to notice the specific internal and external cues that mark genuine momentum — so they can reinforce it rather than accidentally interrupt it. The exercise: recall a peak performance in vivid sensory detail, identify the earliest signal that something was working (the first split-second sensation, posture shift, or sound that preceded the flow state), anchor that signal explicitly. Now you can detect momentum before it crests rather than after it fades.

*Business application:* Sales calls have the same pattern. The first signal that a prospect has genuinely moved — a different quality of attention, a question they stop editing, a posture shift — comes before verbal confirmation. Signal training teaches you to notice it then, not 10 minutes later when you've already moved on.

**Why this book vs. the Grinder/DeLozier source text (#1):** *Turtles All the Way Down* gives you the operating principle. Garratt gives you the sport-specific repetitions. If you read #1 first and understand *why* spatial anchoring works, this book is where you build the habit through structured practice. The Activity boxes are the reps. The sport context is not a metaphor — Garratt's examples are drawn from actual training cycles, which makes the transfer to weekly sales coaching sessions (another performance discipline with preparation, execution, and review phases) immediate.

**The underlying philosophy in one sentence:** Mental preparation is not a supplement to physical preparation — it is the same thing expressed through a different system.

## 7. Peak Performance: Elevate Your Game, Avoid Burnout, and Thrive with the New Science of Success
**Brad Stulberg & Steve Magness · 2017 · ISBN: 9781623367930**

Stulberg (McKinsey consultant) and Magness (Olympic athletics coach) show that the same stress + rest + condition-replication cycle underlies excellence in elite sport and knowledge work. The best modern science treatment of identifying enabling conditions and replicating them deliberately. Cases span Olympic athletes, authors, and executives.

---

## 8. The Power of Full Engagement: Managing Energy, Not Time
**Jim Loehr & Tony Schwartz · 2003 · ISBN: 9780743226752**

Loehr coached 16 world-ranked #1 tennis players. Schwartz applied the same principles to Fortune 500 executives. The core argument: peak performance requires managing physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual energy — and that recovery and reset rituals are as important as peak-state activation. The strongest bridge between elite sport conditioning and corporate performance.

---

## 9. Performing Under Pressure: The Science of Doing Your Best When It Matters Most
**Hendrie Weisinger & J.P. Pawliw-Fry · 2015 · ISBN: 9780804136723**

Based on research with 12,000+ people across Olympic athletes and Fortune 500 executives. Covers peak-state activation before high-stakes moments — directly applicable to pre-call, pre-pitch, and pre-negotiation use of Moment of Excellence anchoring. Pawliw-Fry coaches both the Orlando Magic and Marriott International. The most empirically grounded book on this list.

---

## 10. Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience
**Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi · 1990 · ISBN: 9780061339202**

The theoretical foundation for everything else on this list. Csikszentmihalyi identifies the nine components of flow and explains the conditions coaches must recreate to produce peak states. Cases span rock climbers, chess players, surgeons, and factory workers. Less a how-to than the scientific explanation of why peak-state coaching works — which informs every technique above.
