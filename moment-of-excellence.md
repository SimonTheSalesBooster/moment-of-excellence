# /moment-of-excellence — Find the Peak. Build From There.

> **The technique:** Before coaching what went wrong, locate what went right. The moment where everything clicked is not random — it contains the pattern for replication. Coach from excellence, not from the average.
>
> **Origin:** NLP anchoring (Bandler & Grinder), Maslow's Peak Experiences (1964), Bodynamic Peak Interview. Applied to elite sports by Bernhard Sieber (Austrian Olympic rower, U23 World Champion 2012, Rio 2016) and adapted for sales and strategy coaching by Simon Severino.
>
> **The rowing insight:** Elite rowing coaches rewind to the 3 seconds where the boat moved as one — and coach from that moment. Not from 600 imperfect strokes. From the 3 perfect ones.
>
> **When to use:**
> - Step 0 of every Monday Strategy client session (5-8 min)
> - Opening of any sales coaching call or debrief
> - When a client is stuck, low-energy, or in a spiral of what's not working
> - Before a high-stakes conversation (pitch, negotiation, hiring call)
> - Team debrief after a breakthrough week

## The 5-Move Sequence

Run in order. Never skip to strategy before completing all five.

### Move 1 — Locate the Moment

> "Before we set this week's direction — tell me about ONE moment in the last seven days where you were genuinely at your best. Even briefly. Even for 90 seconds. What day? What conversation? What happened in those specific minutes?"

Push past "good week." Require a specific, located memory.

---

### Move 2 — Re-enter with All Senses

> "Take me into that moment. What do you see? Who's there? What are you hearing? What does it feel like in your body right now?"

Silence. Watch for physiological shift: posture opens, voice slows, energy changes. That is the peak state activating. Do not interrupt it.

---

### Move 3 — Extract the Pattern

> "What was different about how YOU showed up in that moment? Not the situation. You."

Write their answer verbatim. Listen for:
- "I don't know why, but..." — unconscious competence. Dig here.
- Anything credited to luck or others — ask how THEY contributed.
- Conditions appearing in multiple peaks — that is their excellence signature.

Reflect back in their exact words: "So when you [phrase], that's when everything clicked."

---

### Move 4 — Anchor It Forward

> "How do we make that your baseline this week — not your exception?"

Name ONE concrete commitment. Not three. One specific rep, call, or behavior. Write it into the Sprint Dashboard. That is the week's first task.

---

### Move 5 — Set the Anchor Word

> "What one word captures how you felt in that moment?"

("Control." "Clear." "Present." "Calm." "Flow.")

Close with: "This week we're building from [word]. That's your standard."

## The Peak Interview (45-Minute Format)

For quarterly deep-dives, team sessions, or client intake.

**Phase 1 — The Runway (20 min)**

Delay the peak. Explore what came before:
- "What was different about that week?"
- "What were you thinking about the night before?"
- "What did you feel in your body that morning?"

Uncover the enabling conditions. This is the most actionable output.

**Phase 2 — The Peak (10 min)**

Walk through the moment:
- "What were you feeling in your body right then?"
- "What did you do that surprised even yourself?"

**Phase 3 — Extraction (15 min)**

- "Which of those conditions could you recreate next week?"
- "What did YOU specifically do that you don't always do?"

Name the pattern: "I'm hearing you peak when [X condition], [Y behavior], and [Z inner state] are present. That's your excellence signature."

**Output:** Three replicable conditions written on paper. Pre-performance checklist.

## 10 Markers of a True Peak Experience

When 6+ appear, you've found the real one:

1. Rarely shared — carries vulnerability
2. Crystalline recall despite time passing
3. Time felt suspended or expanded
4. Sense of expanded space
5. Heightened sensory awareness
6. Bliss or heart-opening quality
7. Specific physical sensations in the body
8. Deeper conviction or meaning
9. Effortless action (flow)
10. Sense of connectedness — to teammates, the work, something larger

## Why It Works

**State-dependency.** Resources accessed in a peak state are stored with that state. Re-entering the state re-activates the associated competencies — before any advice is given.

**Unconscious competence made conscious.** Peak performers often cannot articulate what they do brilliantly. This surfaces tacit knowledge and makes it teachable and transferable.

**Neuroplasticity.** Full-sensory re-experiencing of a peak activates the same neural pathways as the original event. Repeated retrieval strengthens them.

**Flow conditions (Csikszentmihalyi).** The Peak Interview extracts the conditions that triggered flow — so clients can recreate them deliberately rather than waiting for chance.

**Problem-state vs. excellence-state planning.** Starting with "what's the problem?" activates a deficit-oriented state and every goal is set from there. Starting with excellence activates a resourceful state. Goals planned from strength have higher follow-through.
